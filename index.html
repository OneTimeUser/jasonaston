JASON AStON

Teaching

I’ve spent the last 20 years moving between media, machines, and meaning — designing strange and resonant experiences for screens, spaces, and speculative futures.

I’ve led creative direction for immersive installations, experimental products, and participatory platforms. I’ve taught design as an ethical and expressive practice at NYU, Parsons, and UMD. I’ve co-founded an agency, written code for contraptions, and built systems that think, glitch, and occasionally listen.

My work is often hard to categorize — and that’s the point. Whether I’m working with AI, AR, or real-world materials, I look for how tools can provoke questions rather than just deliver answers.

Currently exploring: generative storytelling, embodied interfaces, and agentic design systems that respond like collaborators. Always prototyping. Always learning.

Let’s build something unusual.
